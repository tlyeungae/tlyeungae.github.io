PhD Candidate, Department of Economics, Università della Svizzera italiana.

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Economics at USI. I received my bachelor's degree in financial engineering from 武漢大學 (WHU) in 2014, a master's degree in economics from HKUST Business School in 2019, and another master's degree in economics and finance from Barcelona School of Economics (UPF) in 2020 with a full tuition waiver. I was a visiting scholar in the Finance Department at the Wharton School (UPenn), January–June 2025, hosted by Prof. Sylvain Catherine.

My research focuses on household finance, with an emphasis on how risk and uncertainty shape decision-making — consumption, saving, labor supply, and portfolio choice in response to shocks and changing economic conditions. I am also interested in the Chinese economy and how its institutional context influences household behavior.

The Price of Housing Uncertainty: Evidence from a Policy Regime Shift

Tai Lo Yeung — solo author

Using 586,000 Beijing apartment listings matched to transactions and platform engagement data, I measure ex-ante valuation uncertainty from hedonic residual dispersion. The uncertainty discount is 2.4× larger after the late-2016 policy pivot — a regime-dependent cross-sectional price of risk.
